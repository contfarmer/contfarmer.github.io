Who is David Mitchell’s wife Victoria Coren Mitchell and how did they meet?

David Mitchell and Victoria Cohen Mitchell are appearing on Celebrity Gogglebox 2020(Picture: Lia Toby/Getty Images for BFI)

David Mitchell one of the guests on tonight’s Celebrity Gogglebox 2020.

The actor and comedian appears alongside his equally funny wife Victoria Coren Mitchell.

Viewers may recognise Coren Mitchell as the host of popular quiz show Only Connect.

But what else is she known for, and how did the pair meet?

Who is Victoria Coren Mitchell?

Victoria Coren Mitchell is a writer, TV presenter, and professional poker player. She also often appears on panel shows such as 8 Out Of 10 Cats and Would I Lie To You?

Coren Mitchell was born in Hammersmith, London, on August 17, 1972 – making her 48-years-old.

Victoria Coren Mitchell is a journalist, TV presenter, and professional poker player (Picture: Mark Cuthbert/UK Press via Getty Images)

Her parents are journalist Alan Coren, and Anne Kasriel.

She has been an avid writer since an early age and had a short story published in Just Seventeen magazine when she was 14-years-old.

She later won a competition in The Daily Telegraph to write a column about teenage life.

Coren Mitchell has also written popular novels, including Love 16 and Once More, With Feeling.

She is also the first woman to win an event on the European Poker Tour, the first player to win both a televised professional tournament (EPT London 2006) and a televised celebrity tournament (Celebrity Poker Club 2005), and the first player to win two European Poker Tour Main Events (EPT London 2006 and EPT Sanremo 2014).

Coren Mitchell has hosted the BBC television quiz show Only Connect since 2008.

She also writes weekly columns for The Observer.

How did David Mitchell and Victoria Coren Mitchell meet?

Victoria once revealed fellow comedian David Baddiel played a major role in getting the couple together.

The couple have David Baddiel to thank for matching them up (Picture: David M. Benett/Dave Benett/Getty Images)

She told the RadioTimes: ‘[Baddiel] specifically said to me, ‘There’s David Mitchell over there, I think you should probably marry him, I’ll get the ball rolling by introducing you’.

‘I thought that was ridiculous but, a couple of hours later, I thought I probably would marry him.’

They had a short relationship in 2007, but rekindled their romance three years later in 2010.

The couple got married in 2012 and had a little girl, Barbara Elizabeth June Mitchell, in 2015.

Celebrity Gogglebox 2020 starts tonight on Channel 4 at 9pm.

“TV review: Us, Only Connect, Grayson Perry’s Big American Road Trip; Harlots”

IN The War of the Roses, that savagely funny morality movie about the break-up of a marriage, the baffled husband asks his wife why she wants a divorce. Says Kathleen Turner, screen spouse of Michael Douglas: “Because. When I watch you eat. When I see you asleep. When I look at you lately, I just want to smash your face in.” Oh dear.

The parting couple in Us (BBC1, Sunday) are far more civilised. “You’re great,” said Connie to Douglas (Saskia Reeves, Tom Hollander). “I just don’t think I can spend my whole life with you.”

Which is the best, most honest way to bring a relationship to an end? Over the course of David Nicholls’ adaptation of his novel I think we are going to enjoy finding out.

Though set in summer as the couple and their son set off on one last hurrah holiday, Us had a nicely melancholic, autumnal air. Reeves and Hollander slipped easily into their roles, her determined to be kind even as she turned his world upside down; him, frantically drowning, not waving, as he tried to work out how to change her mind.

In flashbacks to the early days we saw the way things once were, which made the current distance between the pair more poignant. All the details were just right, from him sobbing at the dump (his “fortress of solitude” said Connie), and the tender awkwardness between father and son. In much the same way that Normal People captured the wonder of young love, Us was a pitch perfect portrait of marriage in middle age, complete with the stuffing knocked out. One to savour.

Only Connect (BBC2, Monday), the quiz show hosted by Victoria Coren Mitchell, was back and jolly pleased about it. Even the continuity announcer was thrilled. “Monday nights haven’t been the same without her,” he said. Don’t know about that, but I do like VCM. Talking about the new series of All Creatures Great and Small, she revealed that Samuel West, who plays Siegfried, was an old boyfriend of hers. “But then who isn’t?” she added. You could never imagine Paxo, whose University Challenge followed, being so endearingly self-deprecating. Long may her quiz mastering, and hosting of Have I Got News for You, continue.

My heart sank at the start of Grayson Perry’s Big American Road Trip (C4, Wednesday, above). He had been to test the temperature of the US as it headed towards the “crunch time” of the 2020 election. So far, so topical. But it was plain within seconds that he was there last summer, before the mass protests over police brutality and Covid. If a week is a long time in British politics, it is an age in American affairs. So much had happened Perry’s film looked as though it would be jarringly dated.

It wasn’t. The introductory narrative covered recent events and Perry’s skills as an interviewer took care of the rest.

His first stop was Atlanta, “the capital of black America” and the subject was race.

Unlike some other celebrity travellers, Perry does not think he is the most interesting person present. Here were people we had not seen before, with original things to say. It was Perry’s job to make that happen. “I always want to talk about the elephant in the room,” he said.

So he asked some young, middle class African-Americans whether black people were happier and more successful the less they had to deal with white people. Gasps. He met three young women performance poets, let them rip and made them laugh. He heard about “white extinction anxiety”, the fear that when white people become a minority they will be as ill-treated as black people are. After an hour you felt that you had genuinely learned something, but that there was such a long way still to go. Any good travel doc should open the door for further exploration.

If only he could have landed an interview with the current commander-in-chief. When Grayson met Donald. A girl can dream.

Did you stick with Harlots (BBC2, Wednesday)? Some people, I know, were put off by the bare scuds, heaving bosoms and ripe humour of the opening episodes of this tale of prostitutes trying to eke a living in 18th century London. There was a lot of mud and boils, too. Especially boils.

Once past that, the story became bleaker and more satisfying, and the performances from the largely female cast, led by Samantha Morton and Lesley Manville as rival madams, came into their own. By the end of series two everyone had to choose which side they were on, and reckonings were due for good deeds and bad.

As was shown by the story of Lady Isabella Fitzwilliam (Liv Tyler no less), kept on an invisible chain by her wicked brother, women in general were treated as second class citizens, their only hope resting in solidarity among themselves. What clever ladies they were, working all that out long before feminism came along. Third series please, Auntie.

“How old is Roger Daltrey from The Who and which films has he been in?”

Roger is best known as a member of The Who (Picture: Getty Images)

Roger Daltrey is a singer-songwriter, actor and film producer, best known as the co-founder and lead singer of the legendary rock band The Who.

The singer is set to appear on tonight’s edition of Celebrity Gogglebox for Stand Up To Cancer, alongside actor and comedian John Bishop.

He boasts an incredible career as The Who have been considered one of the most influential of the 20th century – best known for hits such as My Generation, Pinball Wizard and Baba O’Riley.

Roger later embarked on a solo career, and has released ten studio albums. Some of his signature tracks include Giving It All Away, Written On The Wind and Free Me.

Music asides, Roger is also known for his extensive filmography, appearing in many movies, TV series and advertisements.

But how old is Roger, and what films has he starred in?

How old is Roger Daltrey?

Roger was born in Hammersmith Hospital in London (Picture: Getty Images)

Roger was born on 1 March 1944, which makes him 76 years old.

He was born in Hammersmith Hospital in East Acton, West London, and has two siblings.

Roger married his first wife Jackie Rickman in 1965. The two got divorced in 1968

In 1965, Roger married his first wife, Jackie Rickman, at the age of 20, with the couple welcoming son Simon that same year. In 1967, he became father to another son, Mathias, which was the result of an affair with Swedish model Elisabeth Aronsson.

In 1968, they got divorced, and Roger later met British model Heather Taylor.

Roger has been married to Heather Taylor since 1971 (Picture: Rex)

The two got married in 1971, and have three children together: daughters Rosie Lea and Willow Amber, and son Jamie.

What films has Roger starred in?

Over the course of his career, Roger has starred in many movies, with his first, Tommy, being released in 1975.

Roger starred as the titular character in the 1975 film Tommy, based on The Who’s rock opera album of the same name (Picture: Rbt Stigwood Prods/Hemdale/Kobal)

Tommy portrayed the role of composer Franz Liszt in Lisztomania (Picture: Warner Bros)

Tommy was based on The Who’s rock opera album of the same name, with Roger starring as the titular character, a ‘psychosomatically deaf, mute and blind’ boy who later becomes a world champion at pinball, as well as a religious leader.

Roger also starred in the 1978 horror film The Legacy (Picture: Moviestore/Rex)

The following year, Roger portrayed the role of Franz Liszt in Lisztomania, a musical film based on the life of the famed composer. Roger also helped to provide vocals and lyrics for the movie’s soundtrack.

In 1978, he starred in the horror film The Legacy, about an American couple who are summoned to a mansion in the UK while visiting the country for a work obligation.

Some of Roger’s other well-known film roles include Mack The Knife (1989), Dark Prince: The True Story Of Dracula (2000) and .com For Murder (2001).

And he’ll now be casting his eye over other films and TV shows on Celebrity Gogglebox, joining a star-studded line-up including David Mitchell and Victoria Coren, KSI, Craig David and the Line of Duty cast.
